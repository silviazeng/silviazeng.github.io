My parents visit L.A. now and then, and lately the aunts and uncles too. The Getty Center is always on the list — and none of them read English well enough to get much from it. That's the gap I wanted to close.

There's almost nothing good in Chinese, and the official English guides aren't much help either: they hand you the whole collection and leave you to drown. What my family needed was the opposite — a short list of what's actually worth seeing, and an easy way to find it.

So I made one. It has two parts.

A **read guide** (读·指南): around thirty works worth crossing a room for, each with a short write-up — what it is, where it hangs, and what the Getty paid for it — plus a suggested route so no one walks in circles.

A **walk map** (走·地图): a floor plan for every level of every pavilion, matching the Getty's own layout, with a pin on each piece. Tap a pin to read about it.

  <figcaption style="font-size:0.82rem;color:#6f5a3c;text-align:center;margin-top:0.6rem;line-height:1.4;">The walk map — the West Pavilion's upper floor, where the Impressionists hang. The layout matches the real galleries; every pin is a must-see.</figcaption>
</figure>

Two touches I'm fond of: it notices when you're actually at the Getty, and it leaves a trail of footprints behind you as you walk, so you can see what you've covered. And everything is set in large type — it was made for eyes older than mine.

It's a small thing, useful to a handful of people — and exactly the kind of unquantifiable stuff I like making most.
